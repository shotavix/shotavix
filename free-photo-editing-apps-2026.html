Best Free Photo Editing Apps for Photographers (2026 Guide)

Published: April 12, 2026 by Oli Sa
Professional-quality photo editing doesn't have to cost a thing

You don't need to spend a fortune on software to get professional-looking edits. In 2026, free photo editing apps have evolved to a point where they rival their paid counterparts in features, quality, and ease of use. From powerful desktop RAW editors to convenient mobile apps that fit in your pocket, there's a free solution for every type of photographer.

This guide covers the best free photo editing apps for photographers in 2026, organized by platform and use case. Whether you're editing RAW files on a laptop, touching up portraits on your phone, or designing social media graphics, you'll find the perfect tool here—all without spending a dime.

Why Free Matters: "A monthly plan might look reasonable at first, but over five or 10 years, costs stack fast. Miss a payment, and access to your tools can disappear. Stop subscribing, and editing your own files becomes harder or impossible" . Free software puts you in control.

Best Free Mobile Photo Editing Apps

EDITORS' CHOICE • BEST FREE MOBILE APP
📱 BEST OVERALL MOBILE EDITOR

Snapseed

iOS & Android
Free (no ads, no watermarks)

Snapseed is a one-stop shop for editing your images, an app that really does have it all. Available for both Android and iOS, Snapseed offers tools ranging from basic exposure/colour adjustments to more advanced HDR and Mono conversion features . It's somewhat legendary among photographers for the level of flexibility and control it offers .

The Head Pose option offers a Liquify-style solution to change the direction of gazes in your portraits, while the Selective options will allow for precise adjustments to both JPEG and RAW files . Perspectives can be altered, and distractions can be quickly banished with the Healing tool option. For those feeling more creative, Double Exposures can be created in seconds .

Recently, Google revamped the whole app on iOS with a new interface, more than two dozen new pro tools and beautiful vintage film effects .

RAW SupportYes
Selective EditingPowerful
PriceFree
AdsNone

Pros

  • Free and ad-free
  • Feature-packed, advanced presets
  • Precise adjustments with Raw editing
  • Local adjustment tools for specific areas

Cons

  • Photos saved next to originals
  • Can be too complicated for absolute beginners
  • No recent AI features

Ideal for: Anyone who wants a powerful, completely free mobile editor. "I think you'll struggle to find a better free photo app, full stop" .

📱 BEST FREE ADOBE MOBILE APP

Adobe Photoshop Express

iOS & Android
Free (premium features optional)

With "Photoshop" in its name, it comes as no surprise that this photo editor has it all. Features include layer editing, core exposure and colour adjustments as well as the Heal tool to remove blemishes and distractions . Most of the good Photoshop features are available completely free – no subscription required (though you do need an Adobe account) .

One further set of noteworthy features is Quick Actions, where complex jobs can be completed in one click. Jobs such as adding Denoise to a grainy image, smoothing out skin or even adding digital make-up to your subject . Almost all of Photoshop's power, compressed into a smartphone app .

Ideal for: Quick fixes, denoising, and when you need familiar Adobe tools on mobile .

📱 BEST FREE RAW MOBILE EDITOR

Adobe Lightroom Mobile

iOS & Android
Free (premium features optional)

Photographers know what Lightroom can do and how it works, so the smartphone version will be instantly familiar to those who have used the software on a desktop . The combination of one-click Presets and easy-to-use sliders makes this a great editing app for photographers who need to quickly edit images on the go .

Advanced features include being able to quickly remove Chromatic Aberration or enable Lens Corrections. You can edit RAWs as well as JPEGs, and there are plenty of sharing options available . Adobe Lightroom puts powerful AI photo editor tools in your pocket to make quick edits easy and fun .

Ideal for: Photographers who use Lightroom desktop and want seamless cloud sync, or anyone wanting professional-grade mobile editing .

📱 BEST FOR FILM PRESETS

VSCO

iOS & Android
Free basic version; Plus/Pro subscriptions available

VSCO has quietly amassed one of the best Film Simulation libraries outside of Fujifilm. If you want to drench your images in Kodachrome cool or give them an ethereal monochrome infrared makeover, VSCO is one of the best ways to do it .

The combination of well-made filters with extensive editing tools means you get the best of both worlds, with the ability to one-tap your images into retro style or spend extended time tweaking them just to your liking . If you're a frequent traveller who wants to give your shots a little more pop, VSCO is a brilliant choice .

Ideal for: Creators who love film-inspired presets and want a community-oriented editing experience .

📱 BEST DESIGN + EDITING

Canva

Web / iOS & Android
Free (premium features optional)

Canva is not a traditional photo editing app but rather a design tool that many content creators and self-media enthusiasts rely on. It allows you to create social media posts, cover images, presentations, and marketing materials, offering a wide selection of templates that save considerable prep time .

Editing features include brightness, contrast, color adjustment, cropping, filters, and simple background removal. It's not designed for fine image restoration or professional color grading; its strength lies in quick output, consistent style, and visual appeal .

Ideal for: Social media creators, marketers, and anyone who needs to combine editing with design and text .

📱 BEST AI-POWERED MOBILE EDITOR

PhotoDirector

iOS & Android
Free (premium features optional)

PhotoDirector is one of the best free photo editing apps available. It includes all the tools that everyone loves in Photoshop without the hassle of a steep learning curve . The app connects AI technology with basic image editing functions, providing access to both .

Key features include AI-powered object removal to eliminate unwanted elements, stylish filters and effects, face editing and skin smoothing in just a few clicks, real-time preview of edits, animation effects for photos, and RAW file support .

Ideal for: Users who want AI-powered tools like object removal and skin retouching in a mobile app .

Best Free Desktop Photo Editing Software

EDITORS' CHOICE • BEST FREE DESKTOP
💻 BEST FREE PHOTOSHOP ALTERNATIVE

GIMP (GNU Image Manipulation Program)

Windows, macOS, Linux
Free

GIMP remains the gold standard for free photo editing software. It's a completely free, open-source program that supports layers, masks, and a wide range of plugins . The community has polished the interface significantly over the years .

It's used for complex compositing, and its masking tools are surprisingly robust. With community "PhotoGIMP" patches to make it look like Photoshop, it becomes an incredibly capable tool for free photo editing on Windows .

EDITORS' CHOICE • BEST FREE RAW EDITOR
📸 BEST FREE LIGHTROOM ALTERNATIVE

Darktable

Windows, macOS, Linux
Free

Darktable is a free, open-source RAW photo editor inspired by Lightroom's non-destructive workflow. It offers deep control over exposure, tone curves, color profiles, and masking, supporting a wide range of camera RAW formats . It's frequently recommended on Reddit as the best free RAW photo editor .

The workflow feels very professional: you can adjust exposure, tweak colors, apply lens corrections, and organize your photo library all within one program . On Windows 10, it handles large RAW files smoothly, and the performance has been surprisingly stable even on mid-range laptops . For anyone searching for free photo editing software for Windows, Darktable offers features that rival many paid programs .

📸 ADVANCED FREE RAW PROCESSING

RawTherapee

Windows, macOS, Linux
Free

RawTherapee is a free, open-source RAW processing software focused on maximum image quality and technical control. It provides advanced demosaicing, sharpening, and noise reduction tools, making it popular among photographers who enjoy fine-tuning every aspect of their RAW files .

Though Reddit users often describe RawTherapee as extremely powerful but "not beginner-friendly," it's best suited for technically minded photographers who prioritize image quality over ease of use .

Ideal for: Technically minded photographers who want maximum control over RAW processing .

💻 BEST LIGHTWEIGHT WINDOWS EDITOR

Paint.NET

Windows only
Free

When you need to crop a screenshot or make a quick social media graphic, Paint.NET is the ultimate free Windows photo editor for speed. It launches in about two seconds on a laptop . Don't let the "Paint" in the name fool you—it supports layers, blending modes, and has a massive library of community-made plugins that allow it to handle things like 3D rotation and advanced noise reduction .

It lacks RAW support, so it's not for professional photography, but for everyday "quick and dirty" edits on a Windows machine, it is unbeatable .

Ideal for: Quick edits, resizing, and basic layer work on Windows PCs .

Best Free Online Photo Editors

EDITORS' CHOICE • BEST ONLINE EDITOR
🌐 BEST BROWSER-BASED PHOTOSHOP ALTERNATIVE

Photopea

Any device with a browser
Free (paid for ad-free)

Photopea is a free, surprisingly capable online clone of Adobe Photoshop that replicates many Photoshop features, supports raster and vector editing, and works directly in a browser . It's a lifesaver when you're on a guest PC and need a professional editor instantly .

It's useful for quick fixes, and many users recommend Photopea for convenience. It's handy for quick edits, but most agree it's not suitable for serious RAW workflows .

Ideal for: Quick edits on computers where you can't install software .

🌐 FEATURE-RICH ONLINE EDITOR

Pixlr Editor

Web browser
Free (premium features optional)

Pixlr Editor is a browser-based photo editing software that offers a level of functionality significantly higher than typical online tools. It uses a layer-based interface, supporting masks, blending modes, selection tools, and brushes .

Basic cropping, brightness and color adjustments are smooth, and performance is stable even with higher-resolution photos . It's useful for those who don't want to install software or are using a shared or public computer .

Ideal for: Users who need a full-featured editor in a browser without installation .

How to Choose the Right Free App

Consider Your Platform

  • Mobile editing (iOS/Android): Snapseed is the best all-around choice . For AI features, try PhotoDirector . For design needs, Canva .
  • Desktop editing (Windows/Mac): GIMP for Photoshop-like editing , Darktable for RAW processing , Paint.NET for quick Windows edits .
  • Online editing (any browser): Photopea for Photoshop-like tools , Pixlr for layer-based editing .

Consider Your Needs

  • RAW photo editing: Darktable or RawTherapee .
  • Quick social media edits: Snapseed (mobile) or Photoshop Express .
  • Graphic design + photo editing: Canva .
  • Advanced layer-based compositing: GIMP .
Pro Tip: Before committing to any software, check if it supports your camera's RAW format, whether free versions have watermarks, and if the tool meets your specific workflow needs .

Final Thoughts

The days when "free" meant "basic" are long gone. In 2026, photographers have access to an incredible range of free editing tools that rival paid software in capability and polish. Whether you're editing on a phone, a laptop, or a desktop, there's a free solution waiting for you .

The best free app is the one that fits your workflow and helps you create images you're proud of—without holding your wallet hostage.

Final Challenge: Download one free app from this guide and edit a recent photo with it. You might be surprised at what you can achieve without spending a cent. Your BEST edit is your NEXT edit!
