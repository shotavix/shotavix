Teaching photography one shutter click at a time. I believe great photos come from vision, not gear.

My story

I've been behind a camera for over a decade. What started as a hobby—borrowing my uncle's old film SLR—quickly turned into an obsession. I shot everything: street corners at midnight, family gatherings, landscapes that made me forget to eat. Somewhere along the way, I realized that photography isn't about the gear you own; it's about how you see the world.

After years of working as a commercial photographer and teaching workshops, I built SHOTAVIX to share what I've learned. My mission is simple: demystify photography, cut through the marketing hype, and help you create images you're genuinely proud of. No gatekeeping, no gear shaming—just honest advice and a shared love for the craft.

When I'm not writing or shooting, you'll find me testing vintage lenses, hiking with a medium format film camera, or arguing about the best focal length for portraits. (It's 85mm, obviously.)

How I teach

I believe photography should be accessible, not intimidating. That's why every guide I write starts with the assumption that you're holding a camera for the first time. I avoid jargon when I can, and when I can't, I explain it like I'm talking to a friend over coffee.

My goal isn't to turn you into a copy of me. It's to give you the tools and confidence to develop your own voice. Whether you're shooting with a $500 kit or a $5,000 body, the principles are the same. And I'm here to help you master them.
